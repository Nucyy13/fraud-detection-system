Challenges in Credit Card Fraud Detection

The challenge is to recognize fraudulent credit card transactions so that the customers of credit card companies are not charged for items that they did not purchase.

  • Enormous Data: Enormous data is processed every day, and the model must be fast enough to respond to scams in time.
  • Imbalanced Data: Most transactions (99.8%) are not fraudulent, making it challenging to detect the fraudulent ones.
  • Data Availability: Data is mostly private, posing challenges in availability.
  • Misclassified Data: Not every fraudulent transaction is caught and reported, leading to misclassifications.
  • Adaptive Techniques: Scammers use adaptive techniques against the model.

Approaches to Overcome These Challenges

  • Simple and Fast Model: The model used must be simple and fast to detect anomalies and classify fraudulent transactions quickly.
  • Dealing with Imbalance: Imbalance can be addressed by using proper methods (discussed in the next paragraph).
  • Privacy Protection: To protect user privacy, reduce the dimensionality of the data.
  • Trusted Data Source: Use a more trustworthy source to double-check the data, at least for training the model.
  • Interpretable Model: Make the model simple and interpretable so that it can be quickly adapted to new threats.
